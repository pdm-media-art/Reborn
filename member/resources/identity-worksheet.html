REBORN Method — Phase 3: CREATION

Identity Reset
Worksheet

Addiction is partly an identity problem. You've been living as "the guy who does this." That identity must be consciously dismantled and rebuilt. This worksheet guides you through the most important work in Phase 3: deciding — not discovering — who you are becoming.

How to Use This Worksheet

  • Do this in a quiet space — give it at least 60 uninterrupted minutes
  • Write in the present tense — not "I want to be" but "I am"
  • This is not fantasy — it's a construction blueprint for real behavior change
  • Return to Section 5 (Your Declaration) every morning for 30 days
  • Share your answers with Patrick — this becomes the foundation for your sessions
1

Your Addiction Birthday

Trace the origin — when did this actually start?

Your Addiction Has a Birthday: The behavior didn't come from nowhere. Something happened — a first exposure, an emotional gap, a way to cope that got locked in. Finding that birthday doesn't excuse the behavior. It explains it. And explanation is the first step to change.
2

The Split

Examine both sides of you — the one you're leaving behind and the one you're stepping into.

The Two Men: Inside you right now are two versions of yourself. The old identity that the addiction belongs to. And the new identity that you are building. Recovery is not about fixing the old man — it's about fully becoming the new one. These two men cannot coexist indefinitely.

4

The 5 Pillars

Identity is built on behavior. Define what the new man does consistently.

Identity Follows Action: You don't believe your way to a new identity — you act your way there. Every small action that aligns with the new man strengthens the new neural pathway. Define the behaviors first. The belief follows.

Body & Health

What does this man do with his body daily? Sleep, movement, what he puts in it.

Mind & Focus

How does he protect and train his mind? What does he read, consume, think about?

Relationships

How does he show up for others? What kind of man is he in his closest relationships?

Purpose & Work

What is he building? What work gives him meaning? What does he contribute?

Integrity & Values

What are his non-negotiables? What does he never compromise on, no matter what?

5

Your 3 Core Identity Traits

Choose 3 words that define who you are becoming — then prove them with behaviors.

A Name Changes Everything: When you say "I am disciplined" instead of "I am trying to be more disciplined," your brain processes it differently. These three traits are not aspirations. They are facts you are making true through daily action.
