Week 1 · Days 1–7
SURVIVE &
UNDERSTAND
The first 7 days are the hardest. Your only job is to understand what's happening — not to fix it.

What's happening in your brain right now: Your dopamine system is recalibrating. Withdrawal is real — irritability, brain fog, restlessness. This is not weakness. This is neurochemistry. The urges you feel are your brain desperately seeking the dopamine shortcut it's been trained to use. Every time you don't act on an urge, you weaken that neural pathway. You are literally rewiring yourself — one hour at a time.

Week 2 · Days 8–14
FIND THE
ROOT
The urges are quieting. Now the real work begins: understanding what's underneath the behavior.

The Addiction Birthday: Somewhere in your past — likely before age 18 — there was a moment when your brain first decided that pornography equals safety, comfort, or escape from pain. That moment is your Addiction Birthday. This week, you're going to find it. Not to blame your past, but to understand that your brain was trying to protect you. It just chose the wrong tool.

Week 3 · Days 15–21
REWIRE &
REPLACE
You've survived two weeks and found the root. Now you build new patterns — intentionally.

The brain doesn't delete patterns — it replaces them. You cannot simply "stop" a behavior without giving your brain an alternative dopamine pathway. This week is about building identity-based behaviors that serve the man you're becoming. Not restrictions. Replacements.
