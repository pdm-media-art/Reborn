REBORN Method — Reference

Recovery Knowledge Base

The neuroscience-backed glossary of pornography addiction and recovery. Every term defined clearly — so you can understand what's happening in your brain and why the REBORN Method works.

A B C D S T U
A
Symptom

Anhedonia

The inability to feel pleasure from normally enjoyable activities — a direct consequence of dopamine receptor desensitization caused by chronic pornography use.
B
Symptom

Brain Fog

Cognitive impairment — difficulty concentrating, poor memory, mental sluggishness — linked to prefrontal cortex disruption from chronic pornography use.
C
Behavior

The Chaser Effect

The intense urge to use pornography again shortly after orgasm — driven by a temporary drop in dopamine below baseline levels that compels immediate relief.
D
Neuroscience

DeltaFosB

A transcription factor that accumulates in the brain's reward center with repeated pornography use, permanently sensitizing addiction pathways and deepening compulsive behavior.

 Neuroscience

Dopamine Downregulation

The brain's adaptation to chronic overstimulation — reducing dopamine receptor density and sensitivity, causing emotional numbness and requiring escalating stimulation.
S
Behavior

The Shame Cycle

The self-reinforcing loop where pornography use generates shame, shame creates pain, and the brain returns to pornography to relieve that pain — fueling the addiction it intended to escape.

 Neuroscience

Supernormal Stimulus

An artificial stimulus that exceeds what the brain evolved to process — pornography being the most potent example — producing disproportionate neural responses that override natural reward circuits.
T
Symptom

The Flatline

A recovery phase (typically weeks 2–8) characterized by near-zero libido, emotional numbness, and low motivation as the brain recalibrates dopamine systems after pornography withdrawal.

 Symptom

PIED

Porn-Induced Erectile Dysfunction — the inability to maintain an erection with a real partner due to the brain's recalibration to pornography's supernormal stimulation levels.
U
Behavior

Urge Surfing

A mindfulness-based technique for riding out cravings without acting on them — observing the urge as a wave that builds, peaks, and passes without requiring a response.
